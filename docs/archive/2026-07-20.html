🧠 Data Science Topic of the Day

🎓 RAG — Retrieval-Augmented Generation

Give your LLM a memory it can trust: grounding AI answers in verified, up-to-date facts rather than baked-in model weights.

What It Is

RAG is an architecture that splits the job of 'knowing things' away from the job of 'reasoning and writing.' Instead of relying solely on what a large language model memorised during training, RAG adds a retrieval step: at query time, relevant documents or records are fetched from an external store (a vector database, a knowledge graph, a document corpus) and handed to the LLM as context. The model then generates its answer grounded in that retrieved evidence. The result is a system that is factually fresher, auditable, and far less prone to hallucination — because the answer is anchored to real source material you control.

How It Works

1. INDEXING (offline): Source documents — PDFs, database rows, news articles, financial filings — are chunked into passages, then converted into dense vector embeddings using an encoder model (e.g. OpenAI text-embedding-3, Cohere Embed, or a fine-tuned sentence transformer). Those vectors are stored in a vector database such as Pinecone, Weaviate, pgvector, or Chroma alongside the raw text and metadata. 2. RETRIEVAL (online): When a user submits a query, the same encoder converts it into a query vector. An approximate nearest-neighbour (ANN) search (HNSW, IVF, or similar) finds the top-k most semantically similar passages. Optional re-ranking (e.g. a cross-encoder or BM25 hybrid) refines the shortlist. 3. AUGMENTATION: The retrieved passages are injected into the LLM prompt as a 'context window' alongside the original question and system instructions. 4. GENERATION: The LLM reads the grounded context and produces a response. Because the answer is derived from retrieved chunks, citations back to source documents are straightforward to surface. Key design choices: chunk size and overlap (too small = missing context; too large = diluted signal), embedding model quality, hybrid search (dense + sparse/BM25) for recall, and re-ranking for precision.

When To Use

Use RAG when: (a) your data changes faster than you can retrain a model — e.g. daily company news, quarterly financials, live risk signals; (b) factual accuracy and source auditability are non-negotiable — regulated industries, credit decisions, sales intelligence; (c) you need to personalise responses to proprietary data you cannot bake into a public LLM; (d) hallucination risk is unacceptable and you need 'show your work' explainability. Avoid RAG when the task is pure reasoning over structured data (SQL is cheaper), when the corpus is tiny and fits in a prompt, or when latency budgets are extremely tight and retrieval overhead matters.

🎯 Worked Example — D&B Context

D&B Account Intelligence Agent — 'Why should I call this account today?' A sales rep types a natural-language question into a Salesforce Agentforce widget powered by a D&B RAG pipeline. INDEXING: D&B's 600M+ business records, Paydex payment trend series, Buydex intent signals, corporate family tree changes, and curated news articles are chunked and embedded nightly into a Weaviate vector store. Each chunk carries structured metadata: DUNS number, industry SIC code, record freshness timestamp. RETRIEVAL: The rep's query ('What's the risk profile and buying intent for Acme Corp?') is embedded. ANN search retrieves the top-12 passages — recent Paydex deterioration notes, a corporate parent ownership change flagged three days ago, two Buydex intent spikes in 'cloud ERP' category, and a D&B analyst commentary on the sector. A BM25 hybrid layer ensures the exact DUNS number match is always in the shortlist. GENERATION: The LLM receives the retrieved passages plus a system prompt instructing it to cite every claim with a D&B record ID and freshness date. Output: a structured briefing — 'Acme's Paydex dropped 12 points in 60 days (record #DN-XXXX, refreshed 18 Jul 2026), parent entity changed ownership last week (family tree event #FT-YYYY), and intent signals show 3× baseline activity on cloud ERP (Buydex, week ending 13 Jul). Recommend a risk-aware outreach focused on migration support.' Every fact is traceable to a D&B source record — full explainability, zero hallucination on entity identity because DUNS resolution anchors retrieval before embedding search even runs.
