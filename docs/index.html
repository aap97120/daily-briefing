📝 Data Science Topic of the Day

🎓 RAG (Retrieval Augmented Generation)

Enhancing LLMs with external, verifiable knowledge.

What It Is

Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) is a technique that improves the factual accuracy and relevance of large language models (LLMs) by giving them access to external, up-to-date information. Instead of relying solely on their pre-trained knowledge, RAG systems first retrieve relevant documents or data snippets from a knowledge base. The LLM then uses this retrieved information to formulate its response, significantly reducing hallucinations and providing more grounded answers.

How It Works

The process typically involves two main stages. First, when a user query is received, it's used to search a vast external knowledge base, often stored in a vector database. This search leverages embeddings to find documents semantically similar to the query. Once the most relevant documents are retrieved, they are then provided as additional context to the large language model along with the original user query. The LLM then synthesizes this combined information to generate a more accurate, contextually rich, and verifiable response.

When To Use

RAG is particularly useful when an LLM needs to access proprietary company data, provide answers based on the most current information, or cite specific sources for its claims. It's ideal for applications requiring high factual accuracy, such as customer support chatbots, internal knowledge management systems, or legal and medical information retrieval. Use RAG when the LLM's pre-trained knowledge is insufficient, outdated, or not specific enough for the task at hand.

🎯 Concrete Worked Example (B2B Context)

Imagine a B2B sales enablement platform where sales representatives frequently ask questions about complex product features, pricing tiers, or competitor comparisons. An LLM-powered assistant, without RAG, might provide generic or even incorrect information based on its training data. With RAG, when a sales rep asks, "What are the key differentiators of our 'Enterprise Pro' plan compared to 'Competitor X's' premium offering?", the system first retrieves the latest product documentation, competitor analysis reports, and internal battle cards from the company's knowledge base. These specific, up-to-date documents are then fed to the LLM, allowing it to generate a precise, data-backed comparison, including specific features, pricing advantages, and unique selling points, directly citing the internal resources. This ensures sales reps have accurate, consistent, and verifiable information to share with prospects.
